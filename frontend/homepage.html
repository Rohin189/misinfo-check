How to Spot & Stop Misinformation

Practical habits that raise credibility and curb false amplification.

Check the source

Verify outlet/author; scan the about page and past reporting; prefer established domains.

  • Cross‑verify with reputable outlets
  • Watch for new registrations
  • Confirm masthead & contacts

Verify claims

Use fact‑checkers and primary data; trace quotes to the original.

  • Search exact phrases
  • Find official PDFs/datasets
  • Prefer peer‑reviewed sources

Watch for manipulation

Flag clickbait, crops, deepfakes, and coercive CTAs; check for context cuts.

  • Reverse‑image suspicious visuals
  • Question extraordinary claims
  • Note outrage triggers

Check dates & context

Old items resurface; align time, place, scope and read beyond headlines.

  • Compare timestamps
  • Find full interviews/transcripts
  • Scan methods & samples

Consider incentives

Ask who benefits; follow money, affiliations, and covert campaigns.

  • Trace funding & ties
  • Spot astroturf
  • Identify affiliate/referral links

Pause before sharing

A short pause reduces false spread; verify first, then decide.

  • Read past the headline
  • Check quotes aren’t out‑of‑context
  • Scan replies for corrections
